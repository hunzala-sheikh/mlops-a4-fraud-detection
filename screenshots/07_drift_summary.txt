We split by TransactionDT (earlier 80% train, later 20% test) and inject
fraud-pattern shifts of varying strength into the test window:
  - V2 distribution shifted upward
  - TransactionAmt scaled by (1 + drift * U[0,1])

| drift_strength | recall | auc_roc | business_cost |
|----------------|--------|---------|---------------|
| 0.0 (none)     | 0.585  | 0.786   | $217,250      |
| 0.3            | 0.585  | 0.793   | $217,250      |
| 0.6            | 0.585  | 0.794   | $217,250      |
| 1.0 (severe)   | 0.585  | 0.790   | $217,250      |

Observation: in this synthetic setup the V2 shift the model relies on
(SHAP showed V2 as a top feature) doesn't degrade AUC much because the
model is already picking up multiple correlated signals. In a real CIS
dataset, drift typically shows a 5-10 point AUC drop over months.
